# EV-Dynamic-Pricing-Engine
Dynamic pricing based on operational and environmental factors can increase profit while maintaining acceptable demand levels.

The project main focus would be to answer the following :
1. Peak Period effect : Battery swap demand is significantly higher during peak periods than during off-peak periods. 
2. Weather effect:Rainy weather increases battery swap demand compared to sunny weather.
3. Price sensitivity: Increasing price reduces demand for battery swaps.
4. Queue Length effect : Longer queue lengths are associated with higher demand.
5. Inventory effect : Low battery availability increases stockout risk and reduces potential revenue.
6. Grid Load Effect :High grid load periods should trigger higher recommended prices to reduce demand pressure.
7. Competitor Pricing Effect:When competitors charge higher prices, demand shifts toward our battery swap stations.
8. State of Charge (SOC) Effect:Lower average battery SOC upon arrival leads to higher battery swap demand.
